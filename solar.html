WHY GO SOLAR?

Electricity has become an indispensable necessity to humanity whether it is at home powering your stove to cook or at a company powering machines for business operations. Power outages have made clear the importance of this statement with the increasing frustrated residentials and revenue loss in businesses.

Our solar irradiation (sun power available for converting into electricity) greatly exceeds the average values in Europe, Russia and most of North America (see Appendix A) which are the most successful countries investing in solar technology. Professor DetlevKroger, Senior Researcher and Emeritus Professor in the Department of Mechanical and Mechatronic Engineering at Stellenbosch University claims South Africa is one of the best places in the world to develop solar power. If wet countries like Europe have only about a 1000Wh/m2(irradiation) are owning most of the world’s leading solar power plants because even under those conditions solar still proves to be a solution for successfully reducing electricity bills. South Africa averaging to 2500Wh/m2according to the South African Department of Energy, this represents a huge potential for industry growth with a huge market to tap into.Besides that South Africa’s power system is currently under severe constraints, electricity costs have shown to have risen to more than a 100% in just 7 years.
